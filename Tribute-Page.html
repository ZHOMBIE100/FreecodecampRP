Dr. Kwame Nkrumah

The First Prime Minister and President Of Ghana

Biography Of Dr. Kwame Nkrumah

Dr. Kwame Nkrumah was born in about 1909 in Nkroful, Gold Coast (later known as Ghana). Kwame Nkrumah was raised by his mother and his extended family, who lived together in traditional fashion, with more distant relatives often visiting. He lived a carefree childhood, spent time in the village, in the bush, and on the nearby sea.

Dr. Kwame Nkrumah attended basic and elementry school in Ghana and after sought entry to Lincoln College (through a scholarship programm) March 1935. Nkrumah completed a Bachelor of Arts degree in economics and sociology in 1939. Lincoln then appointed him an assistant lecturer in philosophy, where he was able to earn some money. Nkrumah then went back to school to further himself, which he gained a Bachelor of Theology degree from Lincoln in 1942He earned from Penn the following year a Master of Arts degree in philosophy and a Master of Science in education. While at Penn, Nkrumah worked with the linguist William Everett Welmers, providing the spoken material that formed the basis of the first descriptive grammar of his native Fante dialect of the Akan language

Dr. Kwame Nkrumah as progressing in his life grew more curious in Politics and form his first Political Party named Conventions Peoples Party(CPP) On the 12 of June 1949 that was after he left the UGCC

Dr. Kwame Nkrumah became the first Prime Minister of Ghana on 21 March 1952 under the monarch of Queen Elizabeth the II and President on 1 July 1960

He had one wife and four beautiful children

Dr. Kwame Nkrumah died on 27 April 1972
